### Private Equity Buyouts Slump Amid AI Fears and War
The private equity buyout market is experiencing a significant slump due to concerns over AI integration and the ongoing war in Iran. As of April 6, 2026, private equity sales are down by more than 33% compared to the same period last year. This decline is attributed to the tightening of credit conditions in Europe and the Middle East, which has raised the cost of financing leveraged buyouts.

### Impact on Revenue Projections
Companies involved in recent buyouts are revising their 2026 revenue targets upward only modestly, with an average growth of 10-15% instead of the previously projected 20%. This is due to the uncertainty surrounding AI-driven disruption, which could erode existing business models. For instance, FactSet's Q4 2025 revenue grew by 6.9% year-over-year, while its annual subscription value increased by 5.9% year-over-year.

### Geopolitical Conflict and AI-Related Uncertainty
The war in Iran has tightened credit conditions, making it more challenging for private equity firms to secure financing for leveraged buyouts. Additionally, the rapid expansion of AI adoption is threatening business models, making it difficult for sponsors to price deals accurately. As a result, private equity firms are building larger 'contingency' buffers into their financial models to account for potential disruptions.

### Conclusion
The private equity buyout market is facing significant challenges due to AI integration fears and the ongoing war in Iran. As a result, revenue projections for newly acquired firms are now more conservative, and sponsors are adopting a more cautious approach to deal-making.
