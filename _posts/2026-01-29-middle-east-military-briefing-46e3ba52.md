# Middle East Military Briefing
The Middle East region is currently experiencing significant military deployments and alliances shifts. The United States, Israel, and their allies are strengthening their presence in the region, while Iran, Russia, and China are also increasing their involvement.
## Current Military Deployments
The United States has approximately 40,000-50,000 personnel deployed in the region, with 19 sites and 8 permanent bases. Israel has around 170,000 active-duty personnel, with main bases in Tel Nof, Nevatim, and Palmachim. The United Arab Emirates has around 5,000-7,000 troops, with US-hosted personnel at Al Mithaq in Abu Dhabi.
## Key Alliances
The region is seeing the formation of new alliances, including the Israel-UAE Strategic-Security Partnership and the Trilateral Action Plan between Israel, Greece, and Cyprus. The Saudi-UAE "Arab Shield" and the Iran-Russia-China "Strategic Axis" are also notable alliances in the region.
## Expected Shifts
In the coming months, the region is expected to see a normalization of the US military posture, with the USS *Abraham Lincoln* carrier strike group remaining on station for at least 90 days. Israel and the UAE are also expected to deepen their operational ties, with joint air-defence drills and maritime interdiction exercises scheduled monthly through Q2 2026.
## International Actors
The United States, Russia, and European nations are all playing key roles in the Middle East's military landscape. The US has a significant military presence in the region, while Russia is providing military support to Iran and Syria. European nations, including France, Germany, and the UK, are also involved in the region, with a focus on preventing Iran from acquiring a nuclear weapon.
## Conclusion
The Middle East region is complex and dynamic, with various international actors playing key roles. The region is expected to see continued tensions and potential conflicts, with significant implications for global security and stability.
