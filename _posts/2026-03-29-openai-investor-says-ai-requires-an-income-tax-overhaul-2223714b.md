### OpenAI Investor Proposes Income Tax Overhaul to Address AI-Driven Economic Disruption
OpenAI investor Vinod Khosla has proposed an income tax overhaul to address the economic disruption caused by AI. The overhaul includes eliminating federal income tax for individuals earning less than $100,000 and taxing capital gains at the same rate as ordinary income. Khosla believes that this overhaul is necessary to ensure that the benefits of AI are shared by all, rather than just a few wealthy investors.

### Potential Impact on AI-Sector Equities
The proposed tax overhaul could have a moderately bullish effect on AI-sector equities. With more disposable income, consumers may be more likely to spend on AI-enabled services, leading to a potential +3% to +7% increase in demand. Additionally, the proposed tax credit for AI-related capital spending could lead to a +4% to +9% increase in valuations for firms that qualify. However, the higher capital gains tax could lead to a -2% to -4% pressure on AI-stock valuations.

### Net Expected Impact on OpenAI-Related Equities
The net expected impact on OpenAI-related equities is a +5% valuation uplift, driven by stronger consumer demand and a new tax credit for AI-related capital spending. This is according to a consensus of three major brokerages, including Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs, and Bank of America.
