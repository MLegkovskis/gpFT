# The Big Read: In the Centre of the Storm - What Does the Iran War Mean for Dubai? 
Dubai is uniquely positioned to play a key role in regional diplomatic efforts to resolve the Iran conflict. As the only Gulf state that maintains formal diplomatic relations with both Israel and Iran, the UAE occupies a rare position as a potential bridge between the two nations. 
## Strategic Diplomatic Position 
The UAE's strategic diplomatic position, combined with its sizable economic interdependence with Iran and its recent willingness to use financial tools, makes it an important player in any regional diplomatic effort to resolve the conflict. 
## Economic Lever 
The $3.5 billion trade flow and the financial corridors that run through Dubai give the UAE tangible bargaining chips, and the contemplated asset freeze shows it can also apply pressure. 
## Political Will 
High-level appointments, such as the appointment of former Iranian envoy Khalifa Shaheen al-Marar as UAE Minister of State for Foreign Affairs, demonstrate an official policy of de-escalation rather than military involvement. 
## Regional Coordination 
Dubai works in concert with Saudi Arabia, Oman, and Qatar, forming a Gulf “quiet-track” that can complement formal UN or US-led negotiations. 
## Safety and Security Measures 
In response to the ongoing conflict, Dubai has implemented various safety and security measures to protect its citizens and residents. These measures include emergency alerts, shelter-in-place guidance, enhanced intelligence and liaison efforts, and community-level drills. 
## Conclusion 
Given its dual diplomatic links, sizable economic interdependence with Iran, and recent willingness to use financial tools, Dubai is poised to act as the principal back-channel mediator and economic lever in any regional diplomatic effort to resolve the Iran conflict.
