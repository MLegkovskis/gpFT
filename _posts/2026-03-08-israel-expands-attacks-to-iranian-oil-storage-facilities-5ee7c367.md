# Israel Expands Attacks on Iran to Oil Storage Facilities
The conflict between Israel and Iran has escalated significantly, with Israel expanding its attacks to include Iranian oil storage facilities. This move marks a qualitative shift in the conflict, with Israel targeting Iran's revenue base in addition to its military assets.
## Escalation Timeline
The escalation began on February 28, 2026, when Israel and the U.S. launched a joint campaign against Iran, targeting its leadership, nuclear-related facilities, and ballistic missile sites. On March 7, 2026, Israel struck Iranian oil storage depots, hitting at least three complexes in Tehran and Alborz provinces.
## International Involvement
The conflict has drawn in other regional actors, with Lebanon, the Gulf states, and European countries being affected. The U.K. has deployed Eurofighter squadrons to defend its bases in the region, while Germany and France have urged restraint to avoid a wider conflict.
## Mediation Efforts
Several international bodies and countries have attempted to mediate the conflict, including the IAEA, EU, UN, Russia, China, and Turkey. However, no formal cease-fire or peace process has been launched, and the conflict remains dominated by direct military coordination between Israel and the U.S.
