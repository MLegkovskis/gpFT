### US Attack on Iran: Economic Implications
The recent US attack on Iran has significant economic implications, both domestically and globally. According to estimates, the conflict could cost the US $115-$210 billion in total economic loss, equivalent to a 1.0-1.5 percentage-point drag on real GDP growth and a similar upward pressure on inflation.

The global impact is also substantial, with the IMF expecting the war to reduce global GDP growth by 0.3% (≈ $1.2 trillion) for 2026. A six-month closure of the Strait of Hormuz, which accounts for around 20% of global oil supplies, could lead to a median 0.8% loss (≈ $1.5 trillion) in global output.

The mechanism of the economic impact is largely driven by the disruption of oil supplies and the resulting price spikes. This could trigger higher consumer-price inflation, reduced industrial output, and a 'stagflation-like' environment both in the US and abroad.
