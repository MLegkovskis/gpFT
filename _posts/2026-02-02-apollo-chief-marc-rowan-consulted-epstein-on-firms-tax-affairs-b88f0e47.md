# Apollo CEO Marc Rowan's Epstein Connection Sparks Concerns
The recent revelation that Apollo CEO Marc Rowan consulted Jeffrey Epstein on the firm's tax affairs has raised concerns among investors and regulators. The news could lead to a significant drop in Apollo's stock price, with potential losses of $7-9 billion in market capitalization.
## Investor Relationships and Deal-Making Prospects
The firm's relationships with investors, including major pension funds like CalPERS, may be affected by the scandal. Apollo's ability to secure future deals and raise new capital could also be impacted, with potential reductions in capital commitments and deal-making prospects.
## Governance and Regulatory Scrutiny
Apollo's governance and reputation are under scrutiny, with ongoing investigations and lawsuits adding to the uncertainty. The firm's response to the scandal, including any potential reforms or changes to its governance structure, will be closely watched by investors and regulators.
