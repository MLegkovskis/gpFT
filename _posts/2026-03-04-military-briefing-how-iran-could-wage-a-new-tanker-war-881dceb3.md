# Introduction to the Tanker War 
A new 'tanker war' launched by Iran could have significant implications for regional stability and global oil prices. The Strait of Hormuz is a critical chokepoint, with 21% of global crude oil and 22% of LNG shipments passing through it. 
## Potential Geopolitical Implications 
Any disruption to the Strait of Hormuz could instantly reduce global supply, forcing buyers to source from higher-cost alternatives and creating a sharp price spike. 
## Historical Precedent 
During the 1980-84 Iran-Iraq 'tanker war,' both sides mined the Gulf and attacked merchant vessels, raising insurance premiums by approximately 300% and pushing Brent from $25/bbl (1980) to $35/bbl (1981). 
## Projected Price Reaction 
IG analysts model a 'full-scale' Iran-US-Israel clash and estimate Brent could reach $100-$110/bbl within days of a credible threat to close the Strait. 
## Regional Fiscal Stress 
A prolonged tanker war that depresses prices below $60/bbl would force GCC states to tap sovereign-debt markets, raising the risk of sovereign defaults and limiting their ability to fund diversification plans. 
## Defensive Measures 
Defensive measures such as a persistent multinational naval presence, re-flagging and protected convoys, mine-counter-measure operations, integrated maritime domain awareness, rapid-response strike teams, diplomatic and legal pressure, and economic resilience tools could help mitigate the impact of a tanker war.
