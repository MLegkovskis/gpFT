# Meta's Record Sales and Future Outlook
Meta's Q4 2025 sales were driven by strong ad demand, user-base expansion, and early-stage AI ad products. The company's revenue grew 24% year-over-year to $59.9 billion, beating consensus estimates. 
## Key Drivers of Record Sales
* Strong ad demand: Advertising revenue surged to $58.14 billion, up 24% year-over-year.
* User-base expansion: Daily active users rose 7% to 3.55 billion.
* AI-enhanced ad products: New AI-generated video-ad tools and measurement products improved conversion rates and quickly scaled to multi-billion-dollar run-rates.
## Expected Long-term Impact on the Stock
The record sales and outlook for 2026 have boosted investor confidence, with the stock jumping 10% in after-hours trading. Analysts expect Q1 2026 revenue to beat estimates, and the company's guidance for 2026 capital expenditures suggests a significant investment in AI infrastructure.
## Potential Risks and Benefits
* Continued ad growth: Positive, as long as DAU and ad pricing keep rising.
* AI infrastructure investment: Neutral-to-positive, as short-term earnings margin pressure will likely compress EPS, but AI-driven ad products are projected to lift margins once the infrastructure is in place.
* Reality Labs drag: Negative (limited), as VR/AR continues to be a cash-burner, but the $20 billion 2026 budget is a ~10% reduction from 2025.
* Share-price valuation: Potential upside, as the post-earnings rally shows the market is pricing in confidence that AI spend will be accretive.
