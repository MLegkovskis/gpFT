# Iran's Escalating Attacks on Gulf Energy Infrastructure
## Introduction
Iran has recently intensified its attacks on the Gulf's energy sector, posing a significant threat to global energy security. The campaign, which began in late February 2026, has involved a range of tactics, including drone strikes, missile attacks, and cyber operations.
## Key Events
* February 28, 2026: Coordinated US-Israeli air strikes on Iranian military sites triggered Iranian retaliation.
* March 2, 2026: Drone strike on Qatar's Ras Laffan LNG complex halted LNG output.
* March 4, 2026: Drone attack on Saudi Aramco's Ras Tanura refinery shut the refinery for safety checks.
* March 15, 2026: Massive missile/drone wave on Gulf states; interceptions reported.
## Geopolitical Implications
The ongoing attacks have significant implications for global energy markets, regional security, and the environment. The situation poses a high risk of further escalation, with potential consequences including:
* A global energy-market shock, with oil prices potentially reaching $200 per barrel.
* Strategic chokepoint vulnerability, with the Strait of Hormuz handling approximately 1 million barrels per day.
* Humanitarian and environmental consequences, including large-scale fires, oil spills, and air-quality degradation.
## Conclusion
The escalating attacks on Gulf energy infrastructure pose a significant threat to global energy security and regional stability. The situation requires close monitoring and a coordinated response from regional and international authorities to mitigate the risks and prevent further escalation.
