# Inside the Plan to Kill Ali Khamenei
The successful killing of Ayatollah Ali Khamenei on 1 Mar 2026 would have far-reaching consequences for international relations, Iran's internal power dynamics, and the broader Middle East region.

## Immediate Reactions and Consequences
The operation, carried out by the United States and Israel, would likely be met with widespread condemnation from countries like Russia and China, who have expressed strong opposition to such actions.

## Potential Consequences for International Relations
The killing of Khamenei could lead to a significant escalation of tensions between the United States, Israel, and Iran, with potential repercussions for global oil prices and financial markets.

## Shift in Iran's Internal Power Structure
The event may lead to a power vacuum in Iran, with potential successors including Mojtaba Khamenei, Mohammad Baqr Qalibaf, Ali Shamkhani, and Ali Larijani.

## Regional Security Ripple Effects
The killing of Khamenei could have significant regional security implications, including the potential fragmentation of Tehran-aligned militias in Iraq, intra-factional fights among IRGC-backed groups in Syria and Lebanon, and tightened security postures in Gulf states.

## Strategic Calculations of Adversaries
The United States and other countries would need to carefully consider the potential consequences of such an action, including the risk of uncontrolled proxy escalation and the potential for a chaotic collapse of the Iranian regime.

## Conclusion
The plan to kill Ali Khamenei is a highly complex and sensitive issue, with significant implications for international relations, Iran's internal power dynamics, and the broader Middle East region. The potential consequences of such an action would need to be carefully considered by all parties involved.
