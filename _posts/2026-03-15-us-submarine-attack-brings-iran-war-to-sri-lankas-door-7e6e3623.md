### US Submarine Attack on Iranian Warship: Diplomatic Implications for Sri Lanka
The recent attack by a US submarine on an Iranian warship off the coast of Sri Lanka has significant diplomatic implications for the island nation. With 87 sailors killed and 32 rescued, the incident has sparked a major international crisis.

Sri Lanka has taken a neutral stance, providing humanitarian aid and assistance to the survivors while avoiding taking sides in the conflict. The government has stated that it will 'never hesitate to protect humanity' but will not take sides in the US-Iran/Israel confrontation.

The incident has elevated Sri Lanka's diplomatic workload, as it must simultaneously manage a protest to the United States, placate Iran, reassure China, and leverage its strategic location to maintain security assistance without compromising its long-standing non-aligned posture.

### Regional Security Dynamics
The attack has also raised concerns about regional security dynamics, with China warning that escalation could disrupt shipping lanes vital to its Belt-and-Road trade. Sri Lanka's navy now faces an increased SAR/ESM workload and may request US maritime-domain-awareness support.

### Expected Role of Sri Lankan Government
The Sri Lankan government is expected to play a key role in mitigating the conflict, given its geographical proximity to the affected area. This includes providing immediate SAR and medical response, ensuring maritime security and traffic management, acting as a humanitarian conduit and potential mediator, and fulfilling UNCLOS obligations.

### Conclusion
In conclusion, the US submarine attack on the Iranian warship off the coast of Sri Lanka has significant diplomatic implications for the island nation. Sri Lanka must navigate a complex web of relationships with the US, Iran, and China while maintaining regional stability and providing humanitarian aid to those affected by the conflict.
