# China’s Strategic Gains from the US Situation in Iran
China’s leaders are seeking to extract strategic advantages from the US entanglement in Iran, including:
* **Military learning**: Observing US expeditionary tactics, air-campaign logistics, and coalition coordination to refine PLA joint-operations doctrine.
* **Resource drain on US forces**: A protracted conflict forces the US to keep carrier strike groups, air-refuelers, and logistics in the Gulf, limiting its ability to pivot to the Indo-Pacific and reinforce Taiwan.
* **Diplomatic leverage**: Exploiting global criticism of US “pre-emptive war” to present Beijing as a responsible power willing to mediate, thereby gaining sway in multilateral forums.
* **Energy security**: Preserving access to Gulf oil while the Strait of Hormuz is intermittently blocked; China’s strategic petroleum reserve can absorb short-term shocks.
* **Intelligence gathering**: Deploying satellite, ELINT, and cyber-reconnaissance assets over the Gulf of Oman to map US force posture, feeding into PLA’s anti-access/area-denial calculations.
* **Economic diversification**: Accelerating BRI projects in Gulf-Cooperation Council states, reshaping trade routes away from Tehran while keeping Tehran as a “fallback” partner.

# Implications for US-China and Europe-China Relations
China’s deeper push into Iran could reshape its ties with the US and Europe, with potential implications for strategic partnerships, financial commitments, oil trade volumes, infrastructure projects, military cooperation, and diplomatic balancing.
* **US-China relations**: Likely to deteriorate further as Washington perceives Chinese support for Iran as a direct erosion of its Gulf dominance.
* **Europe-China relations**: Europe will face a “dual-track” dilemma, leveraging Chinese investment for reconstruction while safeguarding its own strategic autonomy and the JCPOA.
