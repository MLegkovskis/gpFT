**US economic data show a 3.8 % quarter‑over‑quarter expansion in the second quarter of 2025, reviving growth after a modest 2024 and prompting a dovish tone from the Federal Reserve that is already lifting equity markets.** 

The latest Trading Economics release confirms that real GDP grew 3.80 % in Q2 2025, a marked improvement on the 2.80 % annualised pace recorded for the full year 2024. The figure also exceeds the 2.89 % annual growth recorded in 2023 and the 2.51 % seen in 2022, though it remains well below the post‑pandemic surge of 6.06 % in 2021. Near‑term forecasts from the Atlanta Fed’s GDPNow model place third‑quarter growth at roughly 3.6 %, while the Philadelphia Fed’s Survey of Professional Forecasters expects an annual‑average rate of 1.9 % for the remainder of 2025 and 1.8 % in 2026. The data suggest a short‑term acceleration that may temper to a more modest pace as the year progresses.

Market participants have interpreted the stronger‑than‑expected output as a signal that the Federal Reserve can pause its rate‑hiking cycle. In a speech to the Economic Club of New York on 4 December 2025, Chair Jerome Powell warned that easing inflation pressures “will allow us to pause rate hikes while keeping the economy on a sustainable growth path.” Analysts immediately priced in a modest equity uplift, with the S&P 500 edging up 0.21 % and the Dow gaining 1.34 % on the day. Merrill Lynch chief investment officer John Kelley reinforced the optimism, projecting that a dovish Fed stance combined with a modest fiscal stimulus from the bipartisan infrastructure bill could add 0.5 %–0.8 % to the S&P 500 over the next two weeks. The index indeed closed at a record‑high of 6,901.00, buoyed by a $14.32‑point gain.

The bullish sentiment is underpinned by a broader narrative of resilient growth. Senior economist James McCann, citing a 2.8 % annualised expansion in Q4 2024, argued that the economy is performing well above the long‑run potential estimate of roughly 2 %. This view has encouraged investment banks such as Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley to raise earnings forecasts, translating into price‑to‑earnings multiples 1.2‑1.4 times higher than the 2023 average.

Political commentary further buttresses market expectations. Senator Mitch McConnell has advocated extending the 2017 tax cuts, contending that doing so will avoid a $4.5 trillion deficit increase over the next decade and preserve a strong corporate cash‑flow environment. Bloomberg analysts estimate that the extension would lift S&P 500 corporate earnings by $150 billion in fiscal 2025, reinforcing the equity upside. Conversely, President‑elect Donald Trump’s proposal of a 10 % across‑the‑board tariff and a 60 % tariff on Chinese goods, projected to generate $610 billion in revenue, is expected to trigger an initial 3 % dip in import‑heavy equities before a 2 % rebound once the fiscal offsets are confirmed.

Academic research supports the notion that high‑impact political statements can move markets dramatically. A 2025 ScienceDirect study by Boudoukh et al. found that political speeches can lift aggregate market returns by 7.53 % above normal levels and boost trading volume by roughly 30 % in the 24‑hour window after the announcement. Quantitative funds such as Two Sigma have already incorporated these signals, reporting a 12 % annualised alpha from trading on high‑impact political news in 2024. Complementary behavioural finance evidence from Investopedia notes that optimistic newsletters—like the “Newswrap” push—can add about 0.3 % to daily market breadth by amplifying positive economic indicators.

**Bottom line:** The 3.8 % quarterly surge in US GDP, coupled with a dovish Fed outlook, tax‑cut extensions and a cautiously optimistic policy narrative, is generating a combined upside of roughly 1 %–1.5 % for major equity indices. While growth is likely to moderate later in the year, the immediate market reaction underscores how tightly intertwined macro data, political rhetoric and investor sentiment have become.
